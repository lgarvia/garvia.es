## 4Ô∏è‚É£ Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)

### üí° Definition

> A market is efficient when **prices fully reflect all available information**.

### üß© Equivalent Statement

> Saying ‚Äúmarkets are efficient‚Äù = saying ‚Äúno arbitrage opportunities exist.‚Äù

### ‚öñÔ∏è But‚Ä¶

- Arbitrage exists temporarily.
    
- Because arbitrage exists ‚Üí markets become efficient.  
    ‚Üí ‚ÄúThere is no arbitrage because there is arbitrage.‚Äù

## 5Ô∏è‚É£ Forms of Market Efficiency

|Form|Information Reflected|Example of Strategy|Works?|Legal?|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Weak**|Historical prices|Technical analysis|‚ùå if market efficient|‚úÖ|
|**Semi-Strong**|Public info (financial statements, news)|Fundamental / Value investing|‚ùå if market efficient|‚úÖ|
|**Strong**|Public + private info|Insider trading|‚ùå if market efficient|‚ùå illegal|

### üîπ Weak Form

- Based only on **past prices**.
    
- Example: **Technical Analysis**, **Candlestick patterns**.
    
- If it ‚Äúworked,‚Äù everyone would use it ‚Üí prices adjust ‚Üí inefficiency disappears.  
    ‚Üí Markets _tend_ to weak-form efficiency.
    

### üîπ Semi-Strong Form

- Uses **public accounting & financial data** (balance sheets, P&L, etc.).
    
- **Fundamental analysis** = estimate intrinsic value ‚Üí compare with market price.
    
- **Value investing** (e.g. Warren Buffett): finds mispriced stocks.
    
    - When Buffett buys ‚Üí prices move ‚Üí market self-corrects.  
        ‚Üí Again, markets _tend_ toward semi-strong efficiency.
        

### üîπ Strong Form

- Incorporates **private information** (insiders).
    
- **Insider trading** = using non-public info to trade.
    
- Illegal under **SEC rules**; enforcement maintains fairness.

## 7Ô∏è‚É£ EMH in Practice: Random Walk

- Stock prices move randomly because all available information is already reflected.
    
- New information ‚Üí unpredictable ‚Üí random.
    
- Trying to forecast systematically ‚Üí no consistent outperformance.
    

---

## 8Ô∏è‚É£ EMH & CAPM: Theoretical Connection

- CAPM assumes markets are efficient ‚Üí returns reflect only **systematic risk (Œ≤)**.
    
- Empirically: anomalies appear (small firms, calendar effects, momentum‚Ä¶).
    
- Some anomalies fade after discovery ‚Üí evidence of **adaptive efficiency**.
    

---

## 9Ô∏è‚É£ From CAPM to Fama‚ÄìFrench

- CAPM: 1 beta (market risk).
    
- **Fama‚ÄìFrench (1993)**: added size (SMB) + value (HML) ‚Üí 3 factors.
    
- Later: 5-factor and multi-beta models (smart beta strategies).  
    ‚Üí Real markets: risk has **many dimensions**, not just Œ≤.
    

---

## üîü Examples of ‚ÄúAnomalies‚Äù

- **January Effect** ‚Äì high December returns, bonuses, then correction.
    
- **Name Effect** ‚Äì firms with ‚ÄúU.S.‚Äù in name overperformed.
    
- **Size Effect** ‚Äì small firms had higher returns.
    
- **Growth vs. Value Effect** ‚Äì persistent patterns.
    

> But anomalies often vanish once published ‚Üí ‚Äúmarkets learn.‚Äù
