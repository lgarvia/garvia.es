## Accounting Basics
- A company‚Äôs **balance sheet** is divided into:  
  - **Assets**: what the company owns or controls.  
  - **Liabilities**: what the company owes (debts, obligations).  
  - **Equity**: what remains for shareholders once liabilities are deducted.  
- The **profit & loss account (P&L)** records income, expenses, and profits over a period.  
- From these two documents, we derive **cash flows** and analyze the company‚Äôs financial health.

## Risk ‚Äì Return ‚Äì Liquidity
The **‚ÄúHoly Trinity‚Äù** of financial markets:  
- **Risk**: uncertainty of outcomes.  
- **Return**: expected benefit from an investment.  
- **Liquidity**: the ease of converting an asset into cash without losing value. Often forgotten (‚ÄúCinderella‚Äù) but crucial.  

All investment decisions balance these three dimensions.  

---

## Key Terms from Session 01
- **Securities**: umbrella term for tradable financial instruments (stocks, bonds, options).  
- **Stocks / Equities**: ownership shares in a company.  
- **Bonds vs. Loans**: both are debt, but bonds are tradable securities.  
- **Mortgages**: loans secured by real estate.  
- **Traders vs. Brokers**: traders act on their own or clients‚Äô behalf, brokers are intermediaries.  
- **Derivatives**: options, futures, CDX ‚Äì contracts based on the value of underlying assets.  
- **Markets**: organized spaces (physical or digital) where securities are issued and traded.  
- **Regulation**: frameworks and rules that guide market functioning.
