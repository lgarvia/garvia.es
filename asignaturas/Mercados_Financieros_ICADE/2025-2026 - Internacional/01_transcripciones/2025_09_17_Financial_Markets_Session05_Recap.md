# Session 5 ‚Äì Recap for Students

## General notes

- Today we worked on **information in financial markets**: what counts as data, what counts as information, and how markets process both.
    
- We also introduced **Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)** and contrasted it with **Behavioral Finance**.
    
- Along the way, we looked at tools (Obsidian, open-source software) and practical examples (Fed policy, HFT, historical cases).
    

---

## Key ideas from class

### 1. Obsidian + Markdown

- Obsidian uses **plain text files** (Markdown) ‚Üí light, fast, and linkable.
    
- Think of it as a **digital Lego**: each note = one brick you can connect.
    
- Why it matters: easier knowledge management than waiting for heavy Word/Docs.
    
- **Open source**: free, community-developed tools that can give you powerful options for notes, video, coding, AI.
    

### 2. Finance = Managing an uncertain future

- We can‚Äôt **predict** the future, but we can **manage** it with tools, instruments, and rules.
    
- Markets exist to connect **savings ‚Üî needs** through instruments (loans, bonds, stocks, derivatives) and institutions (banks, insurers, markets, central banks).
    

### 3. Central banks & context

- Quick look at the Fed (Powell‚Äôs likely move, ‚ÄúRambo movie‚Äù analogy).
    
- Reminder: central banks seek **stability** (ECB ‚Üí inflation ~2%; Fed ‚Üí inflation + jobs).
    

### 4. Information levels & EMH

- **Weak form** ‚Üí only historical prices.
    
    - Tool: **Technical Analysis**.
        
    - Works only until arbitrage removes the inefficiency.
        
- **Semi-strong form** ‚Üí all public info (financial statements, news, guidance).
    
    - Tool: **Fundamental/Value Investing** (Buffett, Graham).
        
    - Harder to beat the market because others are also doing it.
        
- **Strong form** ‚Üí even private info is already reflected.
    
    - **Insider trading** = illegal, prosecuted (SEC and equivalents).
        
    - Key analogy: like driving drunk ‚Äî possible, but not allowed and dangerous.
        

### 5. Arbitrage = the name of the game

- Arbitrage = detect an inefficiency ‚Üí earn money + help the market become more efficient.
    
- Famous stories:
    
    - **Rothschild & Waterloo** (early info ‚Üí big profits).
        
    - **High-Frequency Trading** (fiber cable Chicago‚ÄìNYC, spending millions to shave milliseconds).
        

---

## Takeaways

- **Information is the raw material of finance.**
    
- Markets are not ‚Äúperfect,‚Äù but they **tend toward efficiency** because of arbitrage.
    
- Understanding the difference between **data** and **useful information** is key.
