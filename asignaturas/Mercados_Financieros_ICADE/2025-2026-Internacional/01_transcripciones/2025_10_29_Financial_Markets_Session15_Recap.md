### 2.4 ‚Äî **Introduction to Banks (Preview for Next Class)**

- **What makes a bank different?**  
    The ability to take **deposits** and lend money with a **public license** (like a taxi license).  
    ‚Üí Banking is a **private activity that provides a public service**.
    
- **Products:**
    
    - **Loan:** fixed amount of money, disbursed up front.
        
    - **Credit line:** available if needed; you pay only when you use it.
        
    - **Mortgage:** a loan backed by an asset (house).
        
- **Risks in banking:**
    
    1. **Credit risk:** borrower doesn‚Äôt repay.
        
    2. **Liquidity risk:** lack of cash ‚Üí _ATM ‚Äúout of service‚Äù_ analogy.
        
    3. **Market risk:** losses from changes in **interest rates** or **currencies** (FX).
        
- **Why we focus on bonds:**  
    Bonds are **standardized** and have **secondary markets**, which makes them easier to price, trade, and use for monetary policy transmission.

## üß© 3) Key Concepts to Remember

| Concept                               | Meaning / Application                                                          |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)** | Markets reflect available information; arbitrage keeps prices fair.            |
| **QE / QT**                           | _Quantitative easing/tightening:_ central bank injects or withdraws liquidity. |
| **M0 vs M2**                          | _Monetary base_ (cash + reserves) vs. _money supply_ (adds deposits).          |
| **Holy Trinity**                      | Risk ‚Äì Return ‚Äì Liquidity (the three forces in finance).                       |
| **Map ‚â† Territory**                   | Notes ‚â† reality; knowledge must connect to current events.                     |
| **Data-dependent paradox**            | Acting on data when official data are frozen (e.g., U.S. shutdown).            |
