## üìä 3) What We Covered Today

### 3.1 ‚Äî What Is a Market?

- A **market** is a place (physical or digital) where buyers and sellers exchange goods, services, or financial instruments under specific **rules and supervision**.
    
- Historically, stock exchanges like the **Madrid Stock Exchange** were physical spaces filled with traders shouting orders.
    
- Today, they are **digital platforms** ‚Äî algorithmic, global, and mostly invisible.
    

> **Analogy:** The same transformation that turned cars into ‚Äúplatforms‚Äù (Tesla) or stores into ‚Äúplatforms‚Äù (Amazon) also happened to finance.

**Examples:**

- **NYSE** ‚Äì New York Stock Exchange
    
- **NASDAQ** ‚Äì tech-oriented platform
    
- **BME (Spain)** ‚Äì integrated system for stocks, bonds, and futures
    
- **Korea Exchange (KRX)** ‚Äì modern digital exchange combining multiple markets
    

**Key idea:**

> A financial market is no longer a building ‚Äî it‚Äôs a _technological platform_ that connects needs and savings, buyers and sellers.

---

### 3.2 ‚Äî Financial Assets & Instruments

- A **financial asset** is _an intangible asset_ whose **value comes from a contractual claim**.
    
- It represents a relationship between **issuer and investor** ‚Äî one‚Äôs liability is another‚Äôs asset.
    

**Main features:**

1. **Risk**
    
2. **Return**
    
3. **Liquidity**
    

**Examples:**

|Type|Description|
|---|---|
|**Fixed Income (Bonds)**|Investor lends money for interest; predictable returns.|
|**Equities (Stocks)**|Ownership; higher risk, higher return.|
|**Derivatives**|Value derived from another asset (future, option, swap).|

---

### 3.3 ‚Äî Primary & Secondary Markets

- **Primary market:** when a financial instrument is _issued for the first time_  
    ‚Üí e.g. _IPO (Initial Public Offering)_ for stocks, or _Treasury auctions_ for bonds.
    
- **Secondary market:** where existing instruments are traded later.  
    ‚Üí e.g. buying or selling a bond on the open market.
    

**Example:**

- In the **U.S. Treasury auction**, investors bid for new bonds (primary market).
    
- The next day, they can resell them at market prices (secondary market), and this continuous trading forms the **yield curve** ‚Äî a map of interest rates by maturity.

#### Duration

- Measures **interest rate sensitivity** ‚Äî how much a bond‚Äôs price changes if rates change.
    
- Calculated as a **weighted average of payment times**.
    
- In our example: duration ‚âà **2.74 years**.

### 3.5 ‚Äî Banking vs. Investment Funds

|Aspect|**Bank**|**Investment Fund**|
|---|---|---|
|**What they take**|Deposits|Investor money|
|**Obligation**|Don‚Äôt need to disclose how deposits are used|Must publish **Prospectus**|

### 3.8 ‚Äî Risk, Return & Uncertainty

- **Finance = managing uncertainty**
    
- **Banking = stability**
    
- **Markets = risk and return**
    

**Credit risk:** possibility of default.  
**Interest rate risk:** price moves opposite to yields.  
**Liquidity risk:** inability to sell or withdraw.  
**Systemic risk:** contagion through the system.

Governments are assumed **not to default** ‚Äî their bonds are considered **risk-free assets**, which anchor the entire financial system.
