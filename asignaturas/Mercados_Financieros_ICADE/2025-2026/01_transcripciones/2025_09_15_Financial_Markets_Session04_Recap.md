## General notes

## Executive summary

- **Institutions:** banks & insurers (indirect intermediation), markets (direct intermediation), and central banks (monetary policy).
    
- **Instruments:** deposits, loans/mortgages, bonds, stocks, derivatives.
    
- **Supervisors:** SEC for markets; central banks/authorities for banking.
    
- **Market structure:** **primary vs. secondary** markets; **regulated vs. OTC** venues.
    
- **Information & arbitrage:** information is **asymmetric**; **arbitrage** both earns profits and **pushes markets toward efficiency**.
    
- **Monetary policy now:** expect a well-telegraphed Fed move; the ‚Äúsurprise‚Äù (if any) lives in wording/forward guidance.

## Central banks (what we said & what‚Äôs next)

- Aim: **price stability** (ECB focuses on inflation ‚âà 2%; **Fed has a dual mandate**: inflation + employment).
    
- Policy works through **interest rates**, expectations, and communication.
    
- This week‚Äôs Fed meeting is a ‚ÄúRambo movie‚Äù (highly signalled), but markets still react to the script.
    
- We‚Äôll go deeper in upcoming sessions (tools, transmission, and market impact).
    

## Risk, return, liquidity

- **Return** compensates for **risk**; **liquidity** is what makes things happen (ability to transact quickly at fair prices).
    
- Standardization (contracts, market rules) ‚Üí higher **liquidity** and better comparability.
    

## Information, efficiency, arbitrage

- Not everyone has the same information at the same time ‚Üí **asymmetry**.
    
- The right question isn‚Äôt ‚ÄúAre markets perfect?‚Äù but **‚ÄúWhat do markets do with mistakes?‚Äù**
    
- **Arbitrage** = exploit a mispricing; by doing so you **profit** and **reduce** the mispricing for everyone.
